Overview

Mental well-being is just as important as physical health, yet it remains one of the most overlooked aspects of education and personal development, especially in underprivileged communities. Many children, mentors, and even community members face emotional struggles, stress, and anxiety, but they lack access to psychological support and guidance. Recognizing this urgent need, Dnyansangam Foundation launched Santulan, a mental health initiative dedicated to promoting emotional balance and well-being among students, mentors, and community members.

Santulan, meaning "Balance," aims to create a safe and supportive environment where individuals can express themselves, overcome emotional challenges, and build resilience. The program offers counseling, stress management workshops, emotional intelligence training, and awareness campaigns to ensure that mental health is prioritized and addressed effectively.

Objectives

  • Provide psychological support to students and mentors through expert counseling.
  • Create awareness about mental health issues, emotional well-being, and self-care practices.
  • Develop coping mechanisms to manage stress, anxiety, and personal struggles.
  • Encourage emotional expression through storytelling, group discussions, and creative arts.
  • Foster a community of support where individuals feel heard, valued, and empowered.

Key Activities

1. Free Psychological Counseling Services

Collaborating with psychology experts, counselors, and universities to provide one-on-one and group counseling sessions. Addressing issues such as anger management, emotional distress, academic pressure, family problems, and self-esteem issues. Offering special counseling support for mentors and volunteers, ensuring their emotional well-being as they support students.

2. Mental Health Awareness Workshops

Organizing interactive sessions on emotional intelligence, mindfulness, and self-care techniques. Conducting workshops on stress management, handling failure, and positive thinking. Encouraging open conversations about mental health to break societal stigmas.

3. Expressive Therapy Through Art & Storytelling

Using art, music, and storytelling as therapeutic tools to help students express their emotions. Organizing creative activities like painting, poetry, journaling, and theater to aid emotional healing. Conducting group discussions and experience-sharing circles to create a sense of belonging among participants.

4. Peer Support & Community Engagement

Establishing student and mentor support groups where individuals can share their challenges and receive guidance. Encouraging mentors to actively check on studentsâ€™ mental well-being through regular interactions. Providing training to teachers, parents, and mentors on identifying early signs of mental distress in children and young adults.

5. Yoga, Meditation, and Relaxation Techniques

Introducing simple mindfulness exercises to improve focus and emotional stability. Conducting yoga and guided meditation sessions to help individuals deal with stress. Promoting a holistic approach to mental well-being by integrating physical and mental wellness activities.
